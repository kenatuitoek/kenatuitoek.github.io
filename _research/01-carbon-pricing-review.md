## Abstract

This paper systematically reviews the empirical literature on carbon pricing and trade competitiveness. Following PRISMA guidelines, I identify and synthesise studies examining the effect of carbon taxes and emissions trading systems on export performance in energy-intensive, trade-exposed (EITE) sectors. The majority of studies find small or statistically insignificant competitiveness effects, with free allocation of permits substantially mitigating leakage risks. I discuss implications for the design of the EU's Carbon Border Adjustment Mechanism (CBAM).
